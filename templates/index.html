🚨 The Problem

Wildfire Risk Prediction Challenge

Wildfires pose a significant threat to forests, communities, and ecosystems worldwide. Traditional methods of predicting fire risk are often inadequate, relying on manual assessments and outdated data.

Key Challenges:

  • 💨 Complex interactions between weather variables and fire behavior
  • 🌡️ Difficulty predicting fire intensity from meteorological data
  • ⏰ Need for real-time, accurate predictions to prevent catastrophic fires
  • 📊 Lack of scalable, data-driven solutions for fire risk assessment

💡 Our Solution

Machine Learning-Powered Prediction

Our FWI (Fire Weather Index) prediction model leverages advanced machine learning algorithms to accurately forecast fire risk based on meteorological and fuel conditions.

How It Works:

  • 📈 Analyzes weather data (temperature, humidity, wind speed, rainfall)
  • 🔥 Evaluates fuel conditions (FFMC, DMC, ISI indices)
  • 🎯 Predicts fire intensity using Lasso Regression algorithm
  • ⚡ Provides real-time risk assessments for different regions

📋 Input Variables

🌡️ Temperature

Air temperature (°C) - affects fuel moisture and combustion rate

💧 Relative Humidity

Humidity (%) - inverse relationship with fire risk

💨 Wind Speed

Wind speed (km/h) - critical factor in fire spread

🌧️ Rainfall

Precipitation (mm) - reduces fire risk by increasing moisture

🔥 FFMC Index

Fine Fuel Moisture Code - surface litter moisture

📊 DMC Index

Duff Moisture Code - organic layer moisture
